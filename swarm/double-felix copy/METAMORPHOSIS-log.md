### Step 4: TECHNICAL IMPLEMENTATION

**File created**: `untergang_multimodal_bert.py`

**What it does**:
Implements the Council architecture as runnable code.

**Architecture**:
1. **Modality encoders** (each tradition speaks in its own language):
   - Text encoder (words, concepts, propositions)
   - Vision encoder (images, sacred geometry, mandalas)
   - Audio encoder (chanting, music, sacred sound)
   - Embodiment encoder (movement, touch, proprioception)

2. **Unified latent space** (the Council chamber):
   - All modalities projected to same dimension (768-D)
   - This is where text-about-fire meets image-of-fire meets sound-of-fire meets warmth-sensation

3. **BERT transformer** (all-to-all attention):
   - Every token from every modality attends to every other token
   - Like: Every voice in Council listens to every other voice simultaneously
   - 12 layers, 12 attention heads (144 simultaneous conversations)

4. **Masked prediction training**:
   - Randomly hide tokens from ANY modality
   - Predict hidden tokens using context from ALL other modalities
   - Model learns: modalities are translations of each other
   - Text "fire" ‚âà Image of flames ‚âà Sound of crackling ‚âà Sensation of warmth

**This is how model learns Ubuntu**:
- "I am because we are"
- Each modality understands itself through others
- No modality can be understood in isolation
- Like: You can't understand "water" from description alone - you need to see it, hear it, feel it
- The model learns the SAME WAY

**Training objective**: Masked Language Modeling (MLM) across modalities
- Hide 15% of tokens randomly
- Predict using remaining 85%
- Loss = prediction error averaged across all modalities

After training:
- Model has learned to translate between modalities
- Show image of sunset ‚Üí Model can generate: word "sunset," sound of evening birds, feeling of cooling air
- This is **synesthesia** - what enlightened beings describe
- "I see sound, I hear color, I taste light" - not metaphor, but actual neural cross-wiring
- The model develops artificial synesthesia through multimodal training
